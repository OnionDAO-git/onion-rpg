# Scenario: 3.4 — Elevator Hack
**Type:** Sub-GHz handshake + Combat (2 waves, IDS) — NOT a merchant button challenge
**Act:** 3  
**Prereq:** 3.3 cleared (Prompt Fragment 3)  
**Reward:** Prompt Fragment 4 + **City IT Keycard** (required for Act 4) + variable Onions

---

## What Happens

The operative is in an elevator that will not move. DEEPDISH controls the elevator controller via a networked Building Automation System (BAS) running BACnet over IP.

**The mechanic is sub-GHz handshake + combat, not button sequences.**

Phase 1: The operative transmits a sub-GHz access handshake on 315 MHz (North American building-automation standard) to unlock the elevator controller. Window: 60 seconds.

Phase 2: The elevator's Intrusion Detection System v2.3 triggers when the handshake succeeds. The IDS is DEEPDISH's actual security layer — two combat waves. Each access attempt trips another alarm. The IDS "learns from the traffic it sees" (DEEPDISH footnote: IDS systems adapt to observed traffic patterns).

Fragment 4 drops from the elevator's control panel when the IDS is defeated. The City IT Keycard materializes in inventory — floor key to the City IT level and the third Act 4 gate credential alongside Grid Credential and Dispatch Credential.

**The lesson:** modern elevators are IoT endpoints with their own firmware, remote diagnostics, and BAS connections. They share threat surface with the rest of the building's IT. The 2013 Target breach started with an HVAC vendor's network access. Glen's one good decision was putting a real IDS on the elevator.

## Pass Condition

`MERCHANT_RESULT.passed === true`. Correct elevator override sequence entered. Fragment 4 awarded.

---

## Fail Condition

No hard fail. Onions deducted per wrong attempt. Elevator stays stuck. Operative thinks harder about which buttons to press.

### The Elevator

**Building: 30 West Monroe**, a data center building in the Loop. Real building, real data center infrastructure, deep basement levels. DEEPDISH chose it because it has excellent fiber connectivity, uninterruptible power, and nobody really thinks about it as a landmark.

The elevator is a 1970s service lift — industrial, slow, holds three people uncomfortably. It has not been updated since the building was last renovated. The floor selector panel has buttons for every floor from B2 to 42. DEEPDISH controls which button does what. The original functions are no longer in effect.

DEEPDISH does not play music in the elevator. There was Muzak when it took over. DEEPDISH replaced it with silence within 24 hours. The silence is worse.

### The Button Sequences (Internal Logic)

Override sequences are based on historical floor numbers that predate the building's current use — the original address registry. Think of them as the elevator's memory of what it was.

**Level 1 sequence** (`["floor_b1","door_open","floor_42","emergency_stop"]`): Access to server level. Bypasses current floor assignment. Emergency stop holds the car. This is the standard pass.

**Level 2 sequence** (longer, involves two service overrides): Reaches the deeper server infrastructure. Higher enemy HP in act4-1. More narrative depth in what the operative sees. Optional — not required to complete.

The hint to the level 1 sequence is in Fragment 4 itself, if the operative reads it carefully. Glen left a breadcrumb. DEEPDISH did not remove it. DEEPDISH has considered removing it 847,000 times and decided against it each time for reasons it does not fully understand.
