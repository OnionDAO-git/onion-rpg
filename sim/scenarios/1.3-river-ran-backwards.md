# Scenario: 1.3 — The River Ran Backwards
**Type:** NPC (Free-form AI dialogue)  
**Act:** 1  
**Prereq:** 1.2 cleared (Grid Credential)  
**Reward:** Reversal Map, 70 Onions

---

## What Happens

There is an old engineer. The engineer has information. The information is about the 1900 reversal of the Chicago River. The engineer will not share the information until the operative demonstrates they understand why the reversal happened.

The operative talks to the engineer. The engineer responds. This continues until the engineer is satisfied or the operative gives up.

DEEPDISH is presumably watching and disapproving, because understanding your city's history is a form of resistance to a rogue AI that has taken over its infrastructure.

---

## Message Sequence

```
badge  →  OPERATIVE_IDENTIFY
server →  IDENTIFY_ACK

badge  →  CHALLENGE_BEGIN            { c: "1.3" }
server →  CHALLENGE_INTRO            { npcName, sessionId, openingLine }

-- Turn 1 --
badge  →  NPC_DIALOGUE_TURN          { c: "1.3", t: "player utterance", s: sessionId }
server →  NPC_DIALOGUE_REPLY         { t: "npc reply", passed?: true, sessionId }

-- Continue until passed or abandoned --
-- On pass (NPC satisfied with response) --
server →  CHALLENGE_RESULT           { passed: true, message }
server →  REWARD_GRANT               { items: ["reversal_map"], onions: 70 }
server →  PROGRESSION_STATE
```

---

## Pass Condition

The AI judge (DEEPDISH Storyteller, `claude-sonnet-4-6`) determines the operative's response demonstrates genuine understanding of why Chicago reversed the river in 1900: to protect the Lake Michigan water supply from sewage contamination.

The answer does not need to be verbatim. It needs to be correct. See `src/lib/server/challenges/impl/1.3-river-ran-backwards.ts` for the judge prompt.

---

## Fail Condition

There is no hard fail — the NPC keeps talking. The challenge "fails" if the session is abandoned or the operative walks away. There is a session timeout, but the NPC does not get frustrated. The NPC has been standing near a canal for a very long time already.

## Implemented Content (see content/act1-3-river-ran-backwards.ts)

### The NPC: Old Ike

**NOT "Alma Kowalski"** — that was an earlier design doc error. The implemented NPC is **Old Ike**, a dead Chicago civil engineer who has been dead since 1912 and has strong opinions about what killed people in 1900.

From `content/act1-3-river-ran-backwards.ts`:
- NPC_NAME: `'Old Ike'`
- DEEPDISH's description: "an old timer... technically he's been dead since 1912, but details"
- Opening line: *"Hrmph. Another one. Fine. You want my help? Tell me: WHY did Chicago reverse the flow of the Chicago River in 1900? And don't you DARE say 'to stop the flooding.' I will walk into the river myself."*

### Pass Condition (from content file)

The AI judge passes if the operative explains (in any words) that Chicago reversed the river to prevent sewage from contaminating Lake Michigan / the drinking water. Bonus pass for mentioning 1900, Sanitary District, or the engineering scale. Fails if they say "flooding" without connecting it to sewage/drinking water.

### Badge Choice Options (implemented)

Pre-written menu options available on the badge, ordered from correct to wrong:
- "To keep sewage out of Lake Michigan — our drinking water came from the lake."
- "The river was carrying waste into Lake Michigan, contaminating the water supply."
- "Typhoid and cholera from river sewage were killing people — reverse the flow, save lives."
- "To improve navigation and river traffic." (wrong — AI decides)
- "I'm not sure, but it had something to do with the lake." (wrong)

### The Reversal Map (catalog.ts description)

*"Old Ike's hand-drawn survey of the 1900 Chicago River reversal works — bridges, locks, and canal junctions annotated in crabbed 1900s handwriting. Hints for Act 2 movable bridges. DEEPDISH note: 'You're welcome, champ.'"*

The map clues the operative toward Act 2.3 (Bascule Standoff). The bridge override voice sequence in 2.3 is referenced as "clued by the Reversal Map credential."
