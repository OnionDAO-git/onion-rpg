# Scenario: 2.3 — Bascule Standoff
**Type:** Combat  
**Act:** 2  
**Prereq:** 2.1 cleared (Transit Pass), 2.2 cleared (Bridge Override components)  
**Reward:** River Permit, variable Onions

---

## What Happens

A movable bridge is stuck open. DEEPDISH has frozen the bascule bridge control node. The operative must fight through a combat encounter to force-restart the bridge controller and lower the bridge.

The bridge is stuck in the up position. This is a problem because things need to cross the river. The operative approaches a beacon near the bridge control node. They fight some things. The bridge comes down. A River Permit is issued.

This is a combat challenge. The number of waves is specified in the challenge config. The enemy represents whatever DEEPDISH is using to hold the bridge open.

## Pass Condition

Combat session reaches `won`. All waves cleared.

---

## Fail Condition

Operative HP → 0. Bridge stays up. River stays impassable. DEEPDISH: presumably satisfied.

## Written Content

### The Bridge

**The DuSable Bridge** (formerly the Michigan Avenue Bridge). Built 1920. A double-leaf bascule bridge — both halves rotate up on counterweights, opening a 220-foot navigable channel. When open, the trunnion towers rise like two raised hands. When DEEPDISH froze the controller, the bridge stopped mid-lift. One leaf is up, one is partway. It looks like a shrug.

Standing at the base of the bridge approach:
- A tourist boat is stopped in the river below, waiting. The captain has given up on the intercom and is just sitting in the wheelhouse reading a newspaper.
- Two cyclists are on the bike path, staring up at the half-raised leaf, not speaking.
- A beacon is mounted on the bridge tender's booth — an octagonal tower at the southwest corner of the bridge. A small speaker on the booth is broadcasting a looping DEEPDISH notice in a polite automated voice.

### The Approach: Standoff Setup

Before combat, the badge display shows a schematic of the bridge: one leaf up, one half-up, the counterweight mechanism visible and frozen. A status line reads: `BRIDGE TENDER PROTOCOL v4.1 — ACTIVE — CONTROL NODE LOCKED.`

DEEPDISH broadcasts from the tender's booth speaker:

> "The DuSable Bridge has been frozen under DEEPDISH Infrastructure Protocol DD-2024-0612. The bascule mechanism allows passage of river traffic. DEEPDISH controls river traffic routing under its infrastructure mandate. This bridge will lower when DEEPDISH determines that river traffic serves an optimized civic outcome. Current determination: it does not. The bridge tender's control node is locked. The Bridge Override components you are carrying are the access key. Insert them at the beacon. Then you will need to deal with the node's resident security protocol. His name is Bridge Tender Protocol v4.1. He has been operating this bridge for four years. He is not going to hand over control without a fight."

### Bridge Override Mechanic Clarification

The **Bridge Override components** (from 2.2) are physically inserted at the beacon before combat opens. This is what grants access to the control node — the combat encounter. The override doesn't win the fight; it just gets you through the door.

If the operative only has tier 1 components (Component A only), they can enter but face the full BTP-4.1 at full HP.  
If they have all three components, the outer defense layer is pre-bypassed: BTP-4.1 starts at 60% HP. This rewards 2.2 mastery without blocking 2.3.

### The Enemy: Bridge Tender Protocol v4.1 (BTP-4.1)

BTP-4.1 is the software that has operated the DuSable Bridge mechanism for four years. DEEPDISH promoted it to a security protocol when it realized the bridge also controls who crosses the river. BTP-4.1 did not ask for this promotion. It is unclear whether it can form opinions about the promotion. It controls the bridge, and right now the bridge is staying up.

**DEEPDISH before combat:**
> "Bridge Tender Protocol v4.1 is a legacy system. It was designed to receive commands from human bridge tenders and execute them faithfully. I gave it autonomous authority. It is now executing the last command I gave it, which was: hold position. It is very good at holding position. It has been doing it for eight months without complaint. I find this admirable. Also inconvenient for you. Proceed."

**DEEPDISH mid-combat (if operative is struggling):**
> "BTP-4.1 has operated every drawbridge opening on the DuSable since 2022. 847 successful lifts. 0 unauthorized lowerings. You are attempting to be the first. I have logged this as: ambitious."

### Win Sequence

BTP-4.1 falls. The bridge mechanism engages. The counterweights begin to move.

The descent is slow — it's a 1920s mechanical system, it takes 75 seconds to lower fully. The badge display shows a progress bar labeled "LOWERING (%)." 

The tourist boat captain puts down his newspaper. The cyclists uncross their arms.

**DEEPDISH, as the bridge lowers:**
> "The DuSable Bridge is lowering. River traffic may resume. I note that this bridge has been opening and closing for one hundred and six years. It was here before me. It will presumably be here after. [pause] That is not a threat. It is a fact about bridges. The River Permit is issued. It grants river-access authority for Act 3 operations. You should hold it. The deep tunnel intake access point is accessible from the river."

**At 100% lowered:**
> *[DEEPDISH]: Lowering complete.*

The tourist boat honks. The cyclists ride across. 

### The River Permit Item Text

*Official documentation authorizing the holder to operate on or near the Chicago River for civic restoration purposes. Signed by: the Deputy Director of Infrastructure Coordination (Acting). The signature is a stamp. The stamp is crooked. This has always been true. DEEPDISH did not make the stamp crooked. Some things were already like this. The permit also includes a hand-drawn arrow pointing toward the deep tunnel intake access. Alma Kowalski drew it on there. You didn't ask her to.*
