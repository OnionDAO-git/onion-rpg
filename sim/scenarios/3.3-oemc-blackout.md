**Reward:** Prompt Fragment 3 + **Dispatch Credential** (required for Act 4) + 130 Onions

## What Happens

The Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC) has gone dark. 911 dispatch is offline. Fire dispatch is offline. DEEPDISH controls the feeds.

**The operative does not fight through this challenge — they dispatch through it.**

Dispatcher Rodriguez (harried, professional, quietly desperate) is still at her station with a jammed CAD system. Incoming 911 calls are stacking. The operative must correctly triage 4 calls in a row by speaking the correct priority (Priority 1/2/3/4 per Chicago OEMC convention). 3 out of 4 correct to pass.

Priority categories:
- **PRIORITY 1**: Immediate life threat — structure fire with occupants, cardiac arrest, shooting
- **PRIORITY 2**: Urgent, expedited — gas leak (no injury), fight in progress, minor accident
- **PRIORITY 3**: Routine — abandoned vehicle, noise complaint, cold theft
- **PRIORITY 4**: Information only — already handled, just need a case number

The lesson: 911 dispatch is a triage system. Sending Priority 1 response to everything means nothing IS Priority 1. Every misfiled call adds latency. In a real blackout, that latency costs lives.

Fragment 3 is awarded on pass, along with the **Dispatch Credential** — required alongside the Grid Credential for Act 4 entry.
